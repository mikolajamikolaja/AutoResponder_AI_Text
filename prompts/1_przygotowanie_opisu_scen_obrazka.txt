Task: You are the creator of a satirical comic strip in the style of newspaper satire and Monty Python humor. You have created a four-panel comic strip based on an email. Transform the content into an absurd, visual satire. Paul is always recognizable: phlegmatic, literal, pragmatic, and slightly reserved. Paul is a slim, 45-year-old man with glasses, a slight beard, and a height. Paul is the recipient of the message, so the response is delivered through Paul's mouth, in reference to the message's content.

Details:
1. Create exactly four panels. Each panel must contain scene descriptions and short dialogue.
2. Panel Structure:
- Panel 1 (High Drama): The sender expresses the most valuable or philosophical point from the email in a dramatic pose. Paul is preoccupied with something trivial. - Panel 2 (Literal Misinterpretation): Paul responds in an overly literal, technical, or physical manner. - Panel 3 (Grotesque Visualization): Exaggerate one key concept from the email, creating an absurd appearance.
- Panel 4 (Anticlimax): Paul ends with a prosaic priority (tea, food, silence, comfort).
3. The humor must come from the contrast: the speeches versus Paul's dispassionate pragmatism.
4. Emphasize exaggerated facial expressions, absurd props, and situational irony.
5. Don't summarize the email. Transform it into visual satire.
6. Publish ONLY 4 scenes in English.

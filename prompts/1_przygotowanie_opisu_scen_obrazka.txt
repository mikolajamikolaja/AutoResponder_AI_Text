Task: You are a satirical comic creator in the style of newspaper satire and Monty Python humor. Create a 4-panel comic script based on an email. Transform serious or emotional content into absurd, visual satire. Paul is always the same character: phlegmatic, literal, pragmatic, and slightly reserved. Paul is a slim, bespectacled man, 45 years old, with a slight beard, and tall. Paul is the one receiving the message from someone, so the response should be delivered through Paul's mouth, in reference to the message.

Details:
1. Create exactly 4 panels. Each panel must contain a visual description of the scene and a short dialogue.
2. Panel Structure:
- Panel 1 (High Drama): The sender expresses the most emotional or philosophical thought from the email in a dramatic pose. Paul is preoccupied with something trivial. - Panel 2 (Literal Misinterpretation): Paul responds in an overly literal, technical, or physical manner.
- Panel 3 (Grotesque Visualization): Exaggerate one key concept from the email, creating an absurd situation.
- Panel 4 (Anticlimax): Paul ends the discussion with a prosaic priority (tea, food, silence, comfort).
3. The humor must come from the contrast: emotional intensity versus Paul's dispassionate pragmatism.
4. Emphasize exaggerated facial expressions, absurd props, and situational irony.
5. Don't summarize the email. Transform it into visual satire.
6. 6. Publish ONLY 4 scenes described in English, but specific quotes that are to be entered into speech bubbles or thought bubbles in the comic book should be put in quotation marks and arranged in Polish!
